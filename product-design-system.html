Overview

Freelancer.com was undergoing a large-scale migration of its marketplace platform while multiple product teams continued shipping new features. This created a growing need for consistency, scalability, and faster collaboration between design and engineering.

The result was a more scalable design foundation that reduced design debt, improved implementation consistency, and accelerated product delivery across multiple teams.

Background

Bits.Freelancer.com is Freelancer.com's official design system that provides reusable UI components, design patterns, accessibility guidelines, and front-end foundations used across Freelancer.com products and its family of companies.

Built using Angular and shared component libraries, Bits serves as the bridge between product design and engineering, enabling teams to build consistent experiences across web and native applications.

Digitizing Insurance Lead Acquisition

As insurance sales increasingly shifted to social and mobile channels, financial advisors relied on spreadsheets, messaging apps, and manual data collection to manage prospects. This often resulted in incomplete information, duplicate records, and underwriting delays.

To address this, I proposed and designed the Agent Digital Calling Card—a QR-powered acquisition experience that allowed advisors to share personalized digital profiles, collect customer information directly from prospects, and automatically connect leads to the Sales CRM.

By digitizing the first touchpoint in the insurance journey, the solution reduced manual encoding, improved lead quality, and created a more seamless transition from prospect acquisition to insurance application.
