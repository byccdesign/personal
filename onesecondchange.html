Overview

The Lotus Flower supports women and girls affected by displacement and conflict. One Second Change reframes support as something people can start immediately: a like, comment, repost, or send that helps a success story travel further.

My Role

I shaped the campaign story, art direction, and case-study structure, translating the idea into a LinkedIn-native system that feels like real professional momentum rather than charity messaging.

The visual direction pairs social proof UI with energetic illustration, making refugee women's milestones feel public, celebratory, and shareable.

The Shift

Turn passive applause into proof of possibility.

LinkedIn is already full of career milestones, new roles, and professional congratulations. One Second Change places refugee women's progress in that same feed, so their achievements are seen as ambition, skill, and leadership in motion.

The Insight

Professional validation travels fast.

A single second of engagement can push a post into more networks, more conversations, and more support. The campaign uses the behaviour people already know to create momentum for women whose milestones are often invisible.

The Big Idea

One second can change what gets seen.

Every interaction helps transform a hidden training outcome into a public proof point: women becoming bakers, engineers, entrepreneurs, and leaders in their own communities.

LinkedIn System

A campaign that borrows the language of professional achievement.

The posts look and behave like career announcements, but the milestone belongs to women rebuilding their lives. This makes support feel immediate, credible, and native to the platform.

Proof Posts

Success stories that feel ready to be congratulated.

Each post uses a familiar profile, a real-world job milestone, and celebratory campaign illustration to make support feel like a public endorsement of progress.

OOH

Make the scroll visible in public space.

OOH can extend the feed into transit and city placements, using the same milestone language to ask passersby for one small action: scan, share, and help the next story travel.

Participation

A one-second action people can repeat.

The mechanic is intentionally simple. People do not need to learn a new behaviour; they only need to redirect one they already perform every day toward a story that can open more opportunity.

Partner Moment

Connect engagement back to The Lotus Flower's work.

The campaign can point every post, QR code, and share to a simple hub that explains what one-second actions support: tools, training, visibility, and livelihood pathways for displaced women.

Impact Hub

A lightweight landing page gives every social action a destination, showing the stories behind the milestones and the next practical way to support.

Story Follow-Up

Updates can return to the same feed format, showing what changed after the first wave of attention and keeping the community invested beyond a single post.

The Plan at Scale

A low-friction idea designed for repeat support.
