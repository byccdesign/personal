Looking Beyond Screens to Understand the Process

Rather than treating the initiative as a UI refresh, we mapped the full insurance lifecycle to understand where information, decisions, and approvals broke down between teams. This reframed the work around operational flow: improving how sales, underwriting, back-office, and customer-facing experiences worked together.

Our design team worked closely with advisors, branch staff, operations teams, product owners, and engineers. Through field observations, stakeholder interviews, workflow mapping, usability testing, and process reviews, several recurring themes emerged.

Additional Findings

Key Insights from Our Research

contact_emergency

Advisors Spent More Time Managing Systems Than Serving Customers

Many advisors relied on spreadsheets to track leads because the CRM lacked visibility into application progress and policy statuses. This forced them to constantly switch between tools and manually follow up with internal teams.

folder_info

Data Quality Directly Impacted Underwriting Speed

Customer information was frequently incomplete, inconsistent, or duplicated across systems. Small errors often triggered delays in underwriting reviews and policy issuance.

mobile_2

Mobile Had Become The Primary Working Environment

A large portion of advisors worked remotely and relied on tablets and mobile devices throughout the sales process, yet many workflows were designed primarily for desktop use.

account_tree

Operational Inefficiencies Existed Between Teams, Not Just Interfaces

Many delays occurred when information moved between sales, underwriting, operations, and administrative teams. Improving the interface alone would not solve these issues. The workflow itself needed redesigning.

acquisition

Digitizing Insurance Lead Acquisition

As insurance sales increasingly shifted to social and mobile channels, financial advisors relied on spreadsheets, messaging apps, and manual data collection to manage prospects. This often resulted in incomplete information, duplicate records, and underwriting delays.

To address this, I proposed and designed the Agent Digital Calling Card—a QR-powered acquisition experience that allowed advisors to share personalized digital profiles, collect customer information directly from prospects, and automatically connect leads to the Sales CRM.

By digitizing the first touchpoint in the insurance journey, the solution reduced manual encoding, improved lead quality, and created a more seamless transition from prospect acquisition to insurance application.

Learnings

layers

Digital Transformation Is Not Only A UI Problem

The biggest opportunities came from redesigning operational workflows, handoffs, and business processes behind the interface rather than the screens themselves.

Cross-Functional Alignment Is As Important As Design Execution

Early collaboration with executives, operations, product, engineering, compliance, and external consultants reduced rework and accelerated decision-making throughout the program.

Designing For Systems, Not Only Screens

Reducing manual entry, improving form validation, and increasing visibility into application statuses had a direct impact on underwriting speed and policy issuance outcomes.
